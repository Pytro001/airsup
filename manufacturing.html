Manufacturing

Wild things happening in manufacturing

The most unexpected developments on the factory floor and beyond.

🚀
SpaceX makes a Raptor engine every 4 hours
SpaceX's Raptor engine factory in Hawthorne, CA produces over 1 engine per shift — around 180/month at peak. Each engine has ~100 parts compared to thousands in legacy rocket engines, achieved by eliminating welds through additive manufacturing and casting.
SpaceX · Manufacturing velocity
🏗️
ISRU: manufacturing fuel from Mars air
NASA's MOXIE experiment on Perseverance proved oxygen can be extracted from Mars' CO₂ atmosphere. SpaceX's plan: ship ISRU plants to Mars before humans to pre-produce 30+ tonnes of liquid oxygen for the return trip. The factory arrives before the astronauts.
NASA · Mars ISRU · 2026 prep
🔬
Zero-gravity manufacturing in orbit
Varda Space Industries manufactured pharmaceuticals in orbit aboard a Rocket Lab Photon satellite — then re-entered them to Earth. Microgravity allows crystal structures impossible on Earth, enabling purer drugs and fiber optics with no gravitational distortion during growth.
Varda Space · Commercial orbital manufacturing
🤖
Fully robotic dark factories are real
FANUC in Japan and several Chinese factories now run "lights-out" manufacturing — no humans on the floor, no lighting needed, 24/7 operation. Error rates below 0.001%. The factories monitor themselves. Output is higher at 3am than during day shifts.
FANUC · Lights-out manufacturing
🌑
3D printing a lunar base from moon dust
ESA and NASA are testing sintering lunar regolith (moon dust) into structural bricks using concentrated sunlight or microwave energy. ICON's Vulcan construction system — which 3D prints homes on Earth — has been adapted for lunar ISRU. No material needs to be launched from Earth.
ESA / ICON · Lunar construction
⚡
Solid-state batteries finally being manufactured
Toyota, QuantumScape, and CATL are scaling solid-state battery production for EVs. Energy density 2× lithium-ion, charge in 10 minutes, no fire risk. The manufacturing challenge: creating perfect solid electrolyte layers at nanometer thickness across GWh-scale production.
Toyota / CATL · 2026 scale-up
🧬
Bioprinting organs in space
Microgravity allows 3D bioprinters to print human tissue without scaffolding — cells self-assemble due to the absence of gravity collapse. Redwire Space's BioFabrication Facility on ISS has printed cardiac and kidney tissue. Goal: replacement organs grown in orbit.
Redwire Space · ISS BioFabrication Facility
🏭
AI-designed factories building themselves
Siemens and Nvidia's "digital twin" platforms now auto-generate entire factory layouts optimized for a product — then simulate years of production before a single machine is purchased. Some factories now autonomously reconfigure their own conveyor systems overnight based on next-day order forecasts.
Siemens / Nvidia · Industrial AI
🌊
Underwater foundries and deep-sea mining
The Clarion-Clipperton Zone in the Pacific contains more cobalt, nickel, and manganese than all land-based reserves combined. Companies like The Metals Company are developing robot collectors and seafloor processing units. Raw materials may soon be processed where they're found — thousands of meters down.
The Metals Company · Deep-sea ISRU
