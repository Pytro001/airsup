Manufacturing

🚀
SpaceX makes a Raptor engine every 4 hours
~180 engines/month at peak. Each has ~100 parts — vs thousands in legacy engines — achieved by eliminating welds through additive manufacturing and precision casting.
🤖
Fully robotic dark factories are real
FANUC and several Chinese fabs run "lights-out" 24/7 with zero humans on the floor. Error rates below 0.001%. Some factories autonomously reconfigure conveyor systems overnight based on next-day forecasts.
⚡
Solid-state batteries finally being manufactured
Toyota, QuantumScape, and CATL scaling production in 2026. Energy density 2× lithium-ion, 10-minute charge, no fire risk. The challenge: perfect solid electrolyte layers at nanometer scale across GWh output.

Manufacturing

🌑
3D printing a base from moon dust
ESA and ICON are testing sintering lunar regolith into structural bricks using concentrated sunlight. ICON's Vulcan system — which prints homes on Earth — has been adapted for lunar ISRU. No material launched from Earth.
💧
Water ice = rocket fuel
Confirmed water ice at the lunar south pole could be electrolyzed into hydrogen + oxygen — propellant for deep-space missions. The Moon becomes a fuel depot. Chang'e 7 and LUPEX are mapping exact reserves in 2026.

Manufacturing

🏭
Factories arrive before the astronauts
SpaceX's Mars plan: ship ISRU plants first to pre-produce 30+ tonnes of liquid oxygen from CO₂ atmosphere before any crew arrives. MOXIE on Perseverance already proved it works at small scale.
🧱
Building with Martian regolith
Mars soil contains perchlorates that are toxic but also useful — binders for construction. Researchers at Northwestern have 3D-printed load-bearing structures from simulated Mars regolith. First habitats will likely be half-buried for radiation shielding.
🔬
Microgravity pharma manufacturing
Varda Space manufactured pharmaceuticals in orbit and re-entered them to Earth. Microgravity allows crystal structures impossible to grow under gravity — purer drugs, better fiber optics. Mars gravity (38% of Earth) opens entirely new material science.
