The story of how Air Supply came into existence.

Supi, somewhere between a great idea and a broken supply chain in 2025

Supi had an idea. A real one. The kind that keeps you up at night because you genuinely believe it could help people. He wanted to make something physical, a product that would matter. So he did what everyone does: he started searching for a factory.

What followed was months of cold emails, sales calls that went nowhere, middlemen who promised everything and delivered nothing, and quotes that arrived three weeks late in broken English. Every person he talked to was trying to make money off him. Nobody actually wanted to help him build.

The factories were real. The engineers inside them were talented. But between Supi and those engineers stood an entire layer of salespeople, brokers, and systems designed to slow things down and take a cut. Supi never got his sample. He never got his product off the ground.

He was not alone. Thousands of founders, builders, and companies face the exact same wall every day. Great ideas that never become real products, not because the manufacturing doesn't exist, but because the connection between the two sides is broken.

So Supi built Airsup. Short for Air Supply: the idea that getting what you need to manufacture something should feel as effortless as breathing, not like navigating a maze of gatekeepers.

The vision was simple: let AI do the sourcing. Let it learn everything about your project, find the right factories, talk to them, brief the actual engineer (not a sales rep), and only bring you in when there's a real match.

How Airsup works

1

You tell the AI everything

You come onto the platform and talk to the AI. It learns about you, your company, your product, your requirements, and your timeline. It remembers all of it forever. You never repeat yourself.

2

AI goes out and finds the right factories

The AI searches, scores, and reaches out to manufacturers that match your project. It negotiates terms, briefs the technical team with full context, and figures out if it's a real match. No human involvement needed on either side yet.

3

You get a match with full context

When a factory confirms they can do your project, you get an introduction with everything you need to know: who the engineer is, the quote, the lead time, what the first deliverable will be and when to expect it.

4

You connect directly with the engineer

Both sides already know what the other needs. You connect via WhatsApp with full background on each other. First iteration starts immediately. No sales person in the middle, ever.

5

You pay and manufacturing starts

Transfer payment through the platform. Production begins. Samples get shipped directly to you, no additional approval steps required. Your money is protected the entire time.

All thanks to Supi. He never got to build his product. But he made sure no one else would have to go through what he did. He is just Supiiii.
